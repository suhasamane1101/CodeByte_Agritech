Crop Insurance

  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): This flagship government scheme covers yield losses due to natural calamities like floods, droughts, hailstorms, pests, and diseases. It's mandatory for farmers who take loans for notified crops in notified areas.

  • Restructured Weather-Based Crop Insurance Scheme (RWBCIS): This index-based scheme provides payouts based on deviations from weather parameters like rainfall, temperature, and wind speed, regardless of actual yield loss.

Steps

1. Check Eligibility:
  • Not mandatory for all farmers, only for loanee farmers in notified areas for notified crops.
  • Check the PMFBY website or your local bank/agricultural office for eligibility details.

    • 2. Enrolment Period:
  • Varies depending on your location and crop season.
  • Typically, enrol before the sowing season, often within a specific window (e.g., 15 days to 1 month).

    • 3. Choose Coverage and Pay Premium:
  • Different coverage levels (unit area or individual crop) are available.
  • Premiums are subsidized by the government, you pay a small share.
  • Payment can be made through your bank or designated channels.

    • 4. Get Insured:
  • Once enrolled and premium paid, you receive a policy document confirming your coverage.
  • Keep it safe for claim filing later if needed.

    • 5. File Claim in Case of Loss:
  • Contact your local Gram Panchayat or designated agency if your crop suffers damage due to a covered event.
  • Provide necessary documents and cooperate with the claim assessment process.
  • Claim amount will be credited to your bank account if your claim is approved.
